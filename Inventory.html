Inventory System Project

Role: Solo Developer
Engine: Unreal Engine 5
Focus: RPG-style inventory systems and in-game UI design

For this project, I wanted to learn more about RPG-style inventory systems in Unreal Engine and how to showcase my UI design skills during gameplay, and it ended up being a lot of fun to make. Here is a collection of the systems I have made so far:

  • Player item HUD: Displays player health, stamina, and items.
  • Interaction system: An interactable widget that enables players to pick up items, open chests, and more.
  • Pulling up inventory in game: Player can open inventory at any point in game.
  • Equipping armor, consumables, and ammo: Equipped items show up on player and update in game HUD.
  • Item hover descriptions: Lists stats for items such as durability, weight, value, and more.
  • Sorting items: Players can sort items in inventory by either weight or value.
  • Chest system: Players can open chests and add items to inventory.
  • Dropping items: Players can remove items from inventory by dropping them.
  • Reusable button shader material: Made for dynamic button interface for standard, hover, click, and disabled states.
  • Campfire rest system: A restore point that refills health and creates a checkpoint.
  • Potion equip and healing: Potions can be equipped and used in game to restore player health.
  • Bow and arrow system: Players can launch arrows at enemies and deal damage. Headshots are an automatic kill.
  • Player weight becomes heavier when too many items are in inventory.
  • Icon design and implementation.
  • Weapons and items synced with player locomotion.
  • Archer Enemy AI.
